Linear Congruential Generator

A linear congruential generator (LCG) is an algorithm that yields a sequence of pseudo-randomized numbers calculated with a discontinuous piecewise linear equation. The method represents one of the oldest and best-known pseudorandom number generator algorithms. The theory behind them is relatively easy to understand, and they are easily implemented and fast, especially on computer hardware which can provide modular arithmetic by storage-bit truncation.

What is pseudorandom number generator?

The Linear Congruential Generator (LCG) is one of the oldest and most widely used pseudorandom number generators. It is defined by the recurrence relation:

Xn+1 = (a * Xn + c) mod m

Where:

  • X is the sequence of pseudorandom values
  • m, 0 < m – the "modulus"
  • a, 0 < a < m – the "multiplier"
  • c, 0 ≤ c < m – the "increment"
  • X0, 0 ≤ X0 < m – the "seed" or "start value"

LCGs are fast and easy to implement, but they have some drawbacks. The quality of the generated numbers heavily depends on the choice of the parameters a, c, and m. LCGs also have a relatively short period compared to more advanced generators.

Linear Congruential Generator

Overview

The Linear Congruential Generator (LCG) is one of the oldest and most widely used pseudorandom number generators. It is defined by a recurrence relation:

Xn+1 = (aXn + c) mod m

Where:

  • Xn is the current pseudorandom number
  • Xn+1 is the next pseudorandom number
  • a is the multiplier
  • c is the increment
  • m is the modulus

Steps

  1. Choose the parameters a, c, m, and the seed value X0.
  2. Generate the next pseudorandom number using the recurrence relation.
  3. Repeat step 2 to generate a sequence of pseudorandom numbers.

Example

Let's generate a sequence of pseudorandom numbers using the following parameters:

  • a = 5
  • c = 3
  • m = 16
  • X0 = 7 (seed value)

The sequence will be:

  • X1 = (5 × 7 + 3) mod 16 = 6
  • X2 = (5 × 6 + 3) mod 16 = 1
  • X3 = (5 × 1 + 3) mod 16 = 8
  • X4 = (5 × 8 + 3) mod 16 = 11
  • ...
