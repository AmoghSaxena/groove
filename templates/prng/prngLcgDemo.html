Linear Congruential Generator

Overview

The Linear Congruential Generator (LCG) is one of the oldest and most widely used pseudorandom number generators. It is defined by a recurrence relation:

Xn+1 = (aXn + c) mod m

Where:

  • Xn is the current pseudorandom number
  • Xn+1 is the next pseudorandom number
  • a is the multiplier
  • c is the increment
  • m is the modulus

Steps

  1. Choose the parameters a, c, m, and the seed value X0.
  2. Generate the next pseudorandom number using the recurrence relation.
  3. Repeat step 2 to generate a sequence of pseudorandom numbers.

Example

Let's generate a sequence of pseudorandom numbers using the following parameters:

  • a = 5
  • c = 3
  • m = 16
  • X0 = 7 (seed value)

The sequence will be:

  • X1 = (5 × 7 + 3) mod 16 = 6
  • X2 = (5 × 6 + 3) mod 16 = 1
  • X3 = (5 × 1 + 3) mod 16 = 8
  • X4 = (5 × 8 + 3) mod 16 = 11
  • ...
