True Random Number Generator / Hardware Random Number Generator

Overview

A True Random Number Generator (TRNG), also known as a Hardware Random Number Generator (HRNG) or a Non-Deterministic Random Bit Generator (NRBG), is a device that generates random numbers from a physical process, such as thermal noise, atmospheric noise, or quantum phenomena. Unlike pseudorandom number generators (PRNGs), which use deterministic algorithms, TRNGs produce genuinely random numbers that are unpredictable and have high entropy.

Principles of TRNGs

  • Unpredictability: The generated numbers should be impossible to predict better than by random chance.
  • Uniformity: The numbers should be uniformly distributed, with equal probability for any number to be generated.
  • Independence: The generated numbers should be statistically independent, unrelated to any previously generated numbers.
  • Non-determinism: The generation process should be based on a physical phenomenon that is inherently random and non-deterministic.

TRNG Components

A typical TRNG consists of the following components:

  • Entropy Source: A physical process that produces random noise, such as thermal noise, atmospheric noise, or quantum phenomena.
  • Digitizer: Converts the analog output of the entropy source into a binary representation.
  • Conditioning: Improves the quality of the random bits by removing biases and correlations.
  • Health Tests: Continuously monitor the TRNG's output to ensure proper functioning and detect any failures or attacks.

Example: Generating a Random Number

Suppose we have a TRNG that uses atmospheric noise as its entropy source. The atmospheric noise is sampled and digitized, producing a stream of random bits:

1011010001101011...

After conditioning and health tests, the random bits are ready for use. To generate a random number between 0 and 255, we can take 8 consecutive bits from the stream:

10110100 = 180 (in decimal)

Thus, the TRNG has generated a random number: 180.

   
Important Note

Please note that while this demo illustrates the basic concepts of a TRNG, it is still a simplified representation and may not meet the strict requirements of a true TRNG in terms of entropy quality and unpredictability. Real-world TRNGs often rely on more sophisticated hardware-based entropy sources and post-processing techniques to ensure high-quality randomness.
